# **Transparent Child Benefit Delivery Infrastructure** 

### **Pilot in Education Support Systems in Nepal**

## **1\. Startup Overview and Contact Information**

TechFlowSpace.com is a Kathmandu-based frontier technology startup building transparent, inclusive, and privacy-preserving digital infrastructure for public service delivery. The company specializes in blockchain systems and zero-knowledge cryptographic technologies that enable trusted coordination between governments, NGOs, and communities.

TechFlowSpace works closely with education stakeholders, development organizations, and municipal governments to design open digital systems that improve accountability and service delivery for vulnerable populations. Our focus is on building **open, reusable infrastructure that supports equitable access to essential services for children.**

## **2\. Problem Statement and Impact on Children**

Across Nepal, thousands of children from low-income and marginalized communities rely on external support programs to access and continue education. These programs include scholarships, school meal initiatives, learning materials, and other forms of social assistance.

However, delivery of these benefits is often fragmented. Funding flows through multiple institutions—governments, NGOs, and donors—without a shared infrastructure to verify that resources consistently reach the intended child.

This creates several challenges:

* Limited visibility into whether benefits reach children  
* Administrative burden on schools and local authorities  
* Lack of coordination between programs and organizations  
* Difficulty identifying children at risk of dropping out  
* Limited accountability for service delivery

As a result, some children receive duplicate support while others fall through the cracks entirely. When early warning signals such as declining attendance or missing benefits are not detected in time, children may drop out of school and face increased risk of entering child labor or long-term poverty.

## **3\. Solution Description and Innovation**

TechFlowSpace proposes an **open digital infrastructure that ensures benefits and services intended for vulnerable children are transparently delivered and verifiably received.**

The system enables governments, NGOs, and donors to coordinate support programs through a shared platform that tracks both financial assistance and service delivery while protecting child privacy.

The infrastructure includes the following components:

### **Privacy-Preserving Child Registry**

A secure child registry that allows programs to verify eligibility and enrollment without exposing sensitive personal data. Zero-knowledge cryptographic proofs enable identity validation while preserving privacy.

### **Transparent Benefit Delivery**

Blockchain-based smart contracts manage the conditional disbursement of education benefits such as scholarships, school supplies, or learning support services.

### **Service and Supply Traceability**

The platform tracks the delivery of both financial support and physical resources—including uniforms, textbooks, and learning materials—ensuring that support allocated for children is actually delivered.

### **Teacher and Community Verification**

Teachers and school administrators are provided with simple mobile tools that allow them to confirm the receipt of services and resources. This community verification mechanism strengthens transparency and ensures that benefits reach children in practice, not just in records.

### **Early Warning System for At-Risk Children**

The platform analyzes signals such as declining attendance, interrupted benefit delivery, or teacher alerts to identify children at risk of dropping out. When these signals appear, alerts can be shared with schools, local governments, and NGOs to enable early intervention.

### **Multi-Stakeholder Dashboards**

Interactive dashboards provide visibility to governments, NGOs, and donors, enabling coordinated decision-making and improved targeting of support programs.

### **Open Integration Architecture**

Open APIs allow the platform to integrate with Education Management Information Systems (EMIS) and other public service platforms, enabling interoperability across government and development programs.

## **6\. Digital Public Goods and Global Scalability**

The platform will be released as **open-source digital public infrastructure** designed for reuse by governments and development partners.

While the initial pilot focuses on education benefits in Nepal, the system is designed to support a wide range of child-focused services including:

* school feeding programs  
* health and nutrition support  
* social protection payments  
* humanitarian assistance for children

Many countries across South Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa, and Southeast Asia face similar challenges in coordinating child benefit programs. By building modular and open infrastructure, the system can be adapted and deployed across multiple contexts.

Nepal provides an ideal environment for piloting this approach due to its decentralized governance structure, active NGO ecosystem, and growing digital infrastructure.

## **9\. Conclusion**

TechFlowSpace’s proposed platform addresses a critical gap in the delivery of services for vulnerable children. By combining privacy-preserving identity systems, transparent benefit tracking, and community-based verification, the platform ensures that resources allocated for children are actually delivered and used.

Beginning with education support programs in Nepal, this initiative aims to create **open digital infrastructure that governments and development partners can adopt globally to strengthen accountability and improve outcomes for children.**

With support from UNICEF, TechFlowSpace aims to demonstrate a new model of transparent, child-centered service delivery, ensuring that every child is visible, supported, and able to continue their education with dignity.
